About ALBION

The ALBION Study

Aiginition Longitudinal Biomarkers Investigation of Neurodegeneration.

Mission

Why ALBION?

The mission of ALBION study is to comprehensively investigate the early biological and clinical changes that precede the onset of cognitive decline and dementia. Our goal is to better understand the biological background of cognitive health, contributing to earlier diagnosis, improved prevention strategies, and the development of effective therapeutic interventions.

Ethics approval: The protocol is approved by the Ethics Committee of Aiginiteio Hospital of the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens (NKUA) Protocol Number of Approval: 255, Code of Approval: ΑΔΑ: ΨΘ6Κ46Ψ8Ν2-8ΗΩ.

Study Objectives

  • 1

    Identify early biomarkers of neurodegeneration through the analysis of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF), blood-based and genetic markers, as well as advanced neuroimaging data.

  • 2

    Characterize the natural course of cognitive decline by longitudinally following individuals of age 40 or older.

  • 3

    Examine the interaction between biological, clinical and lifestyle factors by investigating how diet, physical activity, sleep, mental health, and social engagement influence cognitive function and brain health.

Methods

An open, continuously recruiting longitudinal cohort

ALBION is an open, continuously recruiting, longitudinal cohort study with repeated follow-up visits, approximately every 1.5-3 years, allowing to track dynamic changes in cognition, biomarkers, and brain structure over time.

Participants undergo a comprehensive, multidimensional evaluation that includes:

Clinical & Neurological Assessment

Clinical and Neurological Assessment, which includes detailed neurological examination.

Neuropsychological Testing

Extensive neuropsychological testing evaluating different cognitive domains.

Biomarker Assessment

Biomarker Assessment, which includes, lumbar puncture for measurement of CSF biomarkers (Aβ42, total tau, phosphorylated tau) and more, blood sampling for biochemical, genetic and molecular analyses and stool sampling for gut microbiome analysis.

Neuroimaging & Neurophysiology

High-resolution brain Magnetic Reasonance Imaging (MRI), Electroencephalography (EEG).

Lifestyle & Behavioral Assessment

Structured questionnaires on sleep, physical activity, diet, anxiety, depression, and leisure activities and objective monitoring using wearable devices (actigraphy, WatchPAT).

Participants

The sample of the study consists of individuals aged 40 years or older who are either referred by other specialists or self-referred to the cognitive disorders outpatient clinic of a tertiary university hospital. These individuals may have subjective memory complaints or a positive family history, or they may be asymptomatic with a commitment to contributing to medical science.

Inclusion criteria: Only those considered to be cognitively normal or who have at most Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) based on established diagnostic criteria, are included for the purposes of this study. Patients with a diagnosis of dementia are not enrolled, i.e., were excluded from this study, as are patients with neurological, psychiatric, or medical conditions associated with a high risk of cognitive impairment.
