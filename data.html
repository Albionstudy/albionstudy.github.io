Who we study

Participants

The sample of the study consists of individuals aged 40 years or older who are either referred by other specialists or self-referred to the cognitive disorders outpatient clinic of a tertiary university hospital. These individuals may have subjective memory complaints or a positive family history, or they may be asymptomatic with a commitment to contributing to medical science.

Inclusion criteria: Only those considered to be cognitively normal or who have at most Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) based on established diagnostic criteria, are included for the purposes of this study. Patients with a diagnosis of dementia are not enrolled, i.e., were excluded from this study, as are patients with neurological, psychiatric, or medical conditions associated with a high risk of cognitive impairment.

Study design

Protocol

ALBION is a longitudinal study with multiple follow-up visits. Apart from the baseline assessment, follow-up visits are performed either after 1,5 or after 3 years, depending on the age and the diagnosis participants had at their previous visit. Click a visit on the timeline to explore what is the assessments performed at every visit.

Diagnostic Consensus Meetings

Clinicians involved in ALBION perform regular diagnostic consensus meetings where neurologists and neuropsychologists review participants' data to establish diagnoses of dementia and MCI, following international criteria and guidelines. These multidisciplinary meetings ensure a consensus-based approach for accurate clinical and cognitive status diagnoses, drawing on clinical evaluations, neuropsychological testing, and interviews with all researchers involved.

Lifestyle Assessment

Nutritional data

Diet Assessment

Dietary intake is assessed through four 24-h dietary recalls; some of them administered on-site and some by telephone. Three of the recalls refer to weekdays and one to a weekend day. Specifically, trained dieticians ask the participants to report in detail all foods and beverages consumed during the previous day, defined as the timeframe between waking up in the morning and going to bed at night, using the multiple pass approach. Recall data are analyzed in terms of nutrients using the dietary analysis software Nutritionist ProTM (2007, Axxya Systems, Texas, USA).

Physical measurements

Physical Assessment

At each visit, participants undergo a series of standardised physical measurements assessing both anthropometric characteristics and functional performance of the upper and lower extremities.

Anthropometrics

Weight (in kg) and height (in cm) are measured at each visit with a scale and with a portable stadiometer, respectively.

Dynamometer

Physical strength of the upper extremities is estimated using a handgrip electronic dynamometer.

Gait Speed

Physical performance of the lower extremities is assessed with the 4-m measured walk.

Biological samples

Lumbar Puncture — Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF)

A lumbar puncture is performed in the baseline visit and is repeated after 6 years. The procedure of lumbar puncture as well as the collection, processing and storage of Cerebrovascular Fluid (CSF) is conducted according to international guidelines. CSF is primarily processed for biomarkers of neurodegeneration such as Aβ42, T-tau, and p-tau. Apart from this, CSF (for selected participant subsamples) is used for other analyses, including:

  • → Proteomics
  • → Metabolomics
  • → Oxidative Stress Markers: Analyses forTotal antioxidant capacity (TAC), thiobarbituric acid reactive substances (TBARS) and Glutathione (GSH) levels
  • → Inflammation: Analyses for YKL40, sAxl, sTyro3, CRP, sICAM1, sVCAM1 , sTREM2, C1q, C3, C4, Factor B, Factor H, MIF, TNFR1, TNFR2 levels
Biological samples

Blood

Blood samples are collected at the baseline visit and at some of the follow-up visits (e.g., 3, 6 years, and subsequent time points as applicable). Samples are processed according to standardized protocols and stored at −80°C until analysis. Then, the following blood biomarkers (for selected participant subsamples) are measured:

  • → Biomarkers of Neurodegeneration: AD biomarkers, e.g. ptau217, and mixed omics
  • → Exosomes (all and neuronal)
  • → Exosome Proteomics
  • → Metabolomics
  • → Oxidative Stress Markers: Analyses for total antioxidant capacity (TAC), thiobarbituric acid reactive substances (TBARS) and Glutathione (GSH) levels at baseline and at a follow-up visit
  • → Inflammation: Analyses for YKL40, sAxl, sTyro3, CRP, sICAM1, sVCAM1 , sTREM2, C1q, C3, C4, Factor B, Factor H, MIF, TNFR1, TNFR2
  • → Epigenetics: Whole genome epigenetics sequencing
  • → Phospholipoid, PAF, Plasmalogens: Lyso-PAF acetyltransferase activity (PAF-AT), PAF cholinephospho transferase activity (PAF-CPT), PAF levels
Biological samples

Stool

Participants provide a stool sample after receiving a standardized stool collection kit to ensure sterile collection conditions and appropriate storage until delivery to our center. Stool (for selected participant subsamples) are processed for the following analyses:

  • → 16S: Whole metagenome sequencing in stool samples
  • → Metagenomics: Whole metagenome sequencing in stool samples
  • → Metabolomics panel: Targeted metabolomics
Brain imaging

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) & Electroencephalogram (EEG)

Magnetic Resonance Imaging

All participants undergo a whole-brain imaging on a 3T Philips Achieva-Tx MR scanner (Philips, Best, The Netherlands) equipped with an eight-channel head coil. The imaging protocol contains basic clinical and research used sequences. MRI protocol uses traditional MRI sequences and collects information on brain structure:

  • →Atrophy
  • →Communication (DTI)
  • →Function (resting BOLD)

Electroencephalogram

A subsample of the participants are referred for EEG at the baseline visit. EEG recordings are acquired with a 10/20 international system of electrode placement method. All recordings are made with a Micromed SAM 25FO 32-channel headbox (Micromed, Italy) at a sampling rate of 1024 Hz (analogue high pass filter 0.15 Hz).

EEG data are analysed in two ways:

  • →Spectral analysis of posterior dominant rhythm (PDR) for 3s after each eye closure: This is a standard analysis, expected to show a gradual decrease in frequency, amplitude, and stability of the PDR with the progression of Alzheimer’s disease.
  • →A novel non-linear measure of eyes open versus eyes closed EEG synchronization: For this analysis, we are collaborating with the University of Sheffield, which has developed this method.
Objective monitoring

Wearable Devices

Actigraphy (7-day)

Actiwatch 2 (Phillips-Respironics) and MotionWatch8 (CamnTech) are used to record sleep parameters (for selected participant subsamples). The wrist actigraphy is given to participants for 7 consecutive days. The actigraphy device, apart from the activity levels, can produce features regarding the sleep schedule variability the sleep quantity and create sleep quality statistics of the actigraphy sessions of each patient.

The actigraphy device software can identify the daily bed and get-up time and therefore calculate the total time spent in bed. Apart from the total time in bed, the actigraphy device determines:

  • →Total sleep time & onset latency: the time it takes to transition from wakefulness to sleep.
  • →Sleep efficiency: percentage of time spent asleep.
  • →WASO (Wakefulness After Sleep Onset): total number of minutes that a person is awake after the sleep onset.
  • →Number of awakenings: total nocturnal awakenings recorded during the night.
  • →Light exposure: measurements of white light exposure.

WatchPAT™ 300

The WatchPAT™ 300 device has been used (for selected participant subsamples). Watchpat data exports include an automated report with more than 80 variables regarding sleep parameters.
