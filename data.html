Type of Assessments per Visit

ALBION is a longitudinal study with multiple follow-up visits, either after 1,5 or after 3 years, depending on the age and the diagnosis participants had at their previous visit. Click a visit on the timeline below to explore the assessments performed at each visit.

Clinical Assessments

Demographics

Age, sex, education (years), marital status, employment status, address, occupational history.

Neurological Examination

Neurological history & examination.

Diagnostic Consensus Meetings

Clinicians involved in ALBION perform regular diagnostic consensus meetings where neurologists and neuropsychologists review participants' data to establish diagnoses of dementia and MCI, following international criteria and guidelines.

Lifestyle Assessment

Various aspects of lifestyle including leisure time activities, physical activity, sleep and diet are assessed with the use of appropriate questionnaires.

>Subjective cognitive complaintsDepressionAnxiety Leisure time activitiesPhysical activity Sleep

Diet Assessment

Dietary intake is assessed through four 24-h dietary recalls; some of them administered on-site and some by telephone. Specifically, trained dieticians ask the participants to report in detail all foods and beverages consumed during the previous day, using the multiple pass approach. Recall data are analyzed in terms of nutrients using the dietary analysis software Nutritionist ProTM (2007, Axxya Systems, Texas, USA).

Physical Assessment

Anthropometrics

Weight (in kg) and height (in cm) are measured at each visit with a scale and with a portable stadiometer, respectively.

Dynamometer

Physical strength of the upper extremities is estimated using a handgrip electronic dynamometer.

Gait Speed

Physical performance of the lower extremities is assessed with the 4-m measured walk.

Lumbar Puncture — Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF)

Lumbar puncture is performed in the baseline visit and is repeated after 6 years. Cerebrovascular Fluid (CSF) is primarily processed for biomarkers of neurodegeneration such as Aβ42, T-tau, and p-tau. Apart from this, for a subsample CSF is used for other analyses, including:

  • → Proteomics
  • → Metabolomics
  • → Oxidative Stress Markers: Analyses forTotal antioxidant capacity (TAC), thiobarbituric acid reactive substances (TBARS) and Glutathione (GSH) levels
  • → Inflammation: Analyses for YKL40, sAxl, sTyro3, CRP, sICAM1, sVCAM1 , sTREM2, C1q, C3, C4, Factor B, Factor H, MIF, TNFR1, TNFR2 levels

Blood

Blood samples are collected at the baseline visit and at some of the follow-up visits (e.g., 3, 6 years, etc.). The following blood biomarkers are measured for a subsample of participants:

  • → Biomarkers of Neurodegeneration
  • → Exosomes (all and neuronal)
  • → Exosome Proteomics
  • → Metabolomics
  • → Oxidative Stress Markers: Analyses for total antioxidant capacity (TAC), thiobarbituric acid reactive substances (TBARS) and Glutathione (GSH) levels at baseline and at a follow-up visit
  • → Inflammation: Analyses for YKL40, sAxl, sTyro3, CRP, sICAM1, sVCAM1 , sTREM2, C1q, C3, C4, Factor B, Factor H, MIF, TNFR1, TNFR2
  • → Epigenetics: Whole genome epigenetics sequencing
  • → Phospholipoid, PAF, Plasmalogens: Lyso-PAF acetyltransferase activity (PAF-AT), PAF cholinephospho transferase activity (PAF-CPT), PAF levels

Stool

Participants provide a stool sample after receiving a standardized stool collection kit. For a subsample of the participants, stool (for selected participant subsamples) are processed for the following analyses:

  • → 16S: Whole metagenome sequencing in stool samples
  • → Metagenomics: Whole metagenome sequencing in stool samples
  • → Metabolomics panel: Targeted metabolomics

Magnetic Resonance Imaging

MRI

All participants undergo a whole-brain imaging on a 3T Philips Achieva-Tx MR scanner (Philips, Best, The Netherlands) equipped with an eight-channel head coil. The imaging protocol contains basic clinical and research used sequences and, among others, information on the following is extracted:

  • →Atrophy (Global & Atrophy)
  • →Vascular (Infracts, WMH)
  • →Connectivity (DTI)
  • →Function (resting BOLD)

Electroencephalogram

EEG

Electroencephalogram with 19 active electrodes in the “eyes closed” and “eyes open” conditions. Evaluated markers include:

  • →frequency & amplitude of the posterior dominant rhythm (α rhythm)
  • →power of individual electrodes in the δ, θ, α, β & γ frequency bands
  • →interelectrode connectivity in the same bands

Wearable Devices

Actigraphy (7-day)

Actiwatch 2 (Phillips-Respironics) and MotionWatch8 (CamnTech) are used to record sleep parameters (for selected participant subsamples). The wrist actigraphy is given to participants for 7 consecutive days. The actigraphy device, apart from the activity levels, can produce features regarding the sleep schedule variability the sleep quantity and create sleep quality statistics of the actigraphy sessions of each patient.

The actigraphy device software can identify the daily bed and get-up time and therefore calculate the total time spent in bed. Apart from the total time in bed, the actigraphy device determines:

  • →Total sleep time & onset latency: the time it takes to transition from wakefulness to sleep.
  • →Sleep efficiency: percentage of time spent asleep.
  • →WASO (Wakefulness After Sleep Onset): total number of minutes that a person is awake after the sleep onset.
  • →Number of awakenings: total nocturnal awakenings recorded during the night.
  • →Light exposure: measurements of white light exposure.

WatchPAT™ 300

The WatchPAT™ 300 device has been used (for selected participant subsamples). Watchpat data exports include an automated report with more than 80 variables regarding sleep parameters.
