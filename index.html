The ALBION
Study

ALBION (Aiginition Longitudinal Biomarker Investigation Of Neurodegeneration) is designed to address research questions concerning the preclinical and prodromal stage of Alzheimer’s Disease (AD). A basic methodological principle of the present study, lies in the comprehensive neurological and neuropsychological evaluation of study participants, along with collection of bio-samples, validated questionnaires and wearables.

All these sources of information, with some of them obtained at multiple time-points, will allow us to address pathways of disease development and biological mechanisms.

Study at a glance

Clinical Assessments

Participants undergo a comprehensive standardised evaluation, including neurological examination, extensive neuropsychological assessment across five cognitive domains, and diagnostic consensus meetings conducted by neurologists and neuropsychologists following international criteria and guidelines.

Biomarkers

Biomarkers — cerebrospinal fluid, blood, stool, MRI, EEG, wearables (Actigraphy & WatchPAT) and genetic data (DNA & RNA) — are collected longitudinally, providing objective and quantifiable indicators of underlying biological processes related to neurodegeneration, inflammation, oxidative stress, metabolic function, and genetic regulation.

Lifestyle Assessment

Various aspects of lifestyle including subjective cognitive complaints, leisure time activities, physical activity, sleep and diet are assessed with the use of appropriate questionnaires.
